# RTL Advisor V2.3 Implementation Plan

> **Track status — subordinate to MVP V1:** This remains a post-MVP research
> plan for generated calibration data and possible future ML promotion. It is
> not part of the MVP decision path, and it does not gate deterministic MVP
> review, isolated candidate preparation, direct combinational formal proof, or
> the two pinned Yosys/ABC measurements. Where this document conflicts with
> [MVP V1](MVP%20V1.md), MVP V1 controls.

## 1. Objective

V2.3 is a calibration-data and eligibility-score recovery iteration. Its purpose
is to clear the pre-blind 70% balanced actionable-accuracy gate without loosening
any safety constraint and without replacing subsystems that the V2.2 diagnostic
showed are already working.

V2.3 keeps the following V2.1/V2.2 behavior unchanged:

- Kernel-only RTL feature extraction and registered transformation detection.
- PPA regression targets and balanced-profile utility.
- Direction classification and direction confidence gates.
- Qualified-candidate ranking.
- Candidate emission, lint, formal equivalence, and negative-control checks.
- Codex as an explanation-only layer that cannot change the deterministic result.
- The V2.2 global and per-family selective-risk constraints.

V2.3 adds generated calibration RTL for under-supported or poorly separated
families, a stacked eligibility model that can use frozen pre-synthesis PPA
predictions as features, and a versioned OOD refit over the expanded generated
support. It remains diagnostic-only until every calibration and physical gate in
this plan passes.

## 2. Frozen diagnosis and rationale

The V2.2 failure diagnostic is the authoritative design input:

```text
artifacts/models/v22/failure-diagnostics.json
diagnostic semantic hash:
c12ff14887e946054650564adb050270f7d7c6d7bf053ddb992f0032f1a6027f
artifact SHA-256:
c3c5289bd98f939b62e54e8dc909663f1ce3e9f5034fa69c1e092c73c2526d81
```

The diagnostic reproduced the frozen V2.2 policy exactly:

- 230 opportunity cases, 86 covered, and 144 missed.
- 90 recommendations, of which four are harmful.
- 37.39% opportunity recall, 99.43% abstention specificity, 4.44% harmful
  recommendations, and 68.41% balanced actionable accuracy.
- All 86 covered cases selected a measured-best candidate.
- Replacing predicted ranking with measured-oracle ranking at the frozen
  thresholds recovers zero additional cases.
- Of the 144 misses, 131 have no candidate above the family threshold and 13
  belong to unsupported families.
- Only three of the 86 covered recommendations fail the leave-one-topology-out
  OOD overlay.

Therefore V2.3 must improve score separation and family support. It must not
spend the iteration replacing candidate ranking, relaxing thresholds, or broadly
changing OOD behavior.

## 3. Frozen inputs and label boundary

V2.3 may read:

- All V2 and V2.1 calibration RTL, synthesis labels, kernel features, and
  topology descriptors.
- The 2,808 frozen V2.1 candidate rows across 936 topology groups.
- Frozen V2.1 grouped out-of-fold regression and direction predictions.
- Frozen V2.2 grouped out-of-fold family eligibility probabilities.
- V2.1 final regressors and direction models for label-unseen V2.3 expansion
  rows.
- V2.2 final family models for label-unseen V2.3 expansion rows.
- The V2.2 failure diagnostic and passing V2 OpenROAD evidence.

V2.3 may generate and synthesize only the new `calibration-v23` suite during
development. It may not read or create synthesis labels for `heldout-v21`, the
uncreated `heldout-v22`, or the future `heldout-v23` suite while fitting models,
choosing thresholds, or changing implementation. Every reused artifact and
every new suite must be hash-locked.

## 4. Targeted generated calibration expansion

Generate exactly 384 new cases, each containing baseline `v0`, equivalent
candidates `v1`-`v3`, and inequivalent control `n0`:

| Family | New cases | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| arithmetic_resource_sharing | 96 | Raise four-case opportunity support and cover narrow datapaths. |
| adder_reduction_association | 48 | Improve the weakest supported-family score separation. |
| comparator_selection | 64 | Raise nine-case opportunity support and improve precision. |
| decode_factoring | 48 | Add positive and boundary structures beyond the nearly exhausted domain. |
| mux_placement | 48 | Improve score separation across add/subtract/compare structures. |
| priority_selection | 32 | Add hard negatives to reduce the four observed false positives. |
| width_signedness | 48 | Improve precision for compare and mixed-signedness opportunities. |
| **Total** | **384** | **1,152 candidate rows after validation and synthesis.** |

Do not add V2.3 calibration cases for `popcount_saturation`, whose V2.2 score
separation and policy are perfect, or `variable_shift`, which has zero
opportunities across 104 frozen calibration cases. Popcount retains its frozen
V2.2 model and threshold. Variable shift retains constant abstention.

### 4.1 Expanded topology domains

All topologies must remain disjoint from V2, V2.1, and any future blind suite.
Reuse currently unused legal V2.1 tuples first. Expand only the families that do
not have enough remaining tuples for calibration plus an eight-case blind
reserve:

- Resource sharing adds widths `4`, `6`, `10`, `14`, `20`, and `28` and branch
  count `5`. Multiplication remains limited to widths at most eight and at most
  three branches.
- Decode factoring adds opcode widths `5`, `7`, and `9`; match counts `6`, `10`,
  `14`, `18`, and `20`; reuse counts `3` and `6`; and result widths `12` and
  `24`. Exhaustive-decode and duplicate-mask cases remain illegal.
- Mux placement adds widths `10`, `14`, `20`, and `28` and fan-in `5`.
  Multiplication remains limited to widths at most eight and at most three
  inputs.

All declared values must appear in the selected suite. Selection must retain at
least 85% pairwise coverage over the eligible new-value space, or the generator
must fail before RTL is written.

### 4.2 Frozen selection policy

Use deterministic seed `20260717`. Within each family, reserve 25% of its budget
for greedy pairwise coverage. Use a smoothed categorical opportunity-propensity
model trained only on V2/V2.1 calibration labels to select the remaining 75%:

- Resource sharing, adder association, comparator selection, decode factoring,
  mux placement, and width/signedness: half of the total family budget from the
  highest predicted-opportunity candidates and one quarter nearest the predicted
  0.5 boundary.
- Priority selection: half from the lowest predicted-opportunity candidates and
  one quarter nearest the predicted 0.5 boundary, so the expansion deliberately
  supplies hard negatives.

Resolve ties with a stable hash of seed, family, and topology. Freeze descriptors
and topology signatures before generation. Selection cannot be changed after any
V2.3 synthesis label exists.

### 4.3 Required case validation

Every new case must pass:

- PySlang and Verilator lint.
- Yosys formal equivalence for `v1`-`v3` against `v0`.
- Formal rejection of `n0`.
- Nangate45 synthesis for `v0`-`v3` with the pinned Liberty file.
- Complete provenance and immutable RTL, proof, and synthesis hashes.

Any failed case remains recorded and cannot be silently replaced after its
synthesis result is visible. The model-training table includes only cases that
complete the full contract; an incomplete family allocation blocks training.

## 5. Stacked eligibility features

Keep the existing 49 kernel-only candidate features. Append only values available
before synthesis at live inference time:

- Predicted delay, area, and cell-count improvement from the frozen V2.1
  regressors.
- Balanced predicted utility.
- Timing-branch margin:
  `min(predicted_delay - 3, predicted_area + 10)`.
- Area-branch margin:
  `min(predicted_area - 5, predicted_delay + 2)`.
- Overall eligibility margin: the maximum of the timing and area branch margins.
- Frozen V2.1 direction labels and their confidence margins for delay, area, and
  cell count.
- Frozen V2.2 family eligibility probability.
- Within-case predicted-utility rank and distance from the best predicted
  candidate.

Persist an ordered stacked-feature schema and hash it. Do not use measured PPA,
case identifiers, topology signatures, corpus split, OOD verdicts, or any blind
metadata as model features.

### 5.1 Leakage prevention

- Existing 936-case reference rows use only the frozen V2.1 and V2.2 grouped
  out-of-fold base predictions.
- New V2.3 expansion rows use final V2.1/V2.2 base models, which were frozen
  before the new labels existed.
- No row may receive a base prediction from a model trained on that row's PPA or
  eligibility label.
- Cross-validation groups all three templates from one topology together.

Add a mechanical provenance check for every stacked value. Training stops on a
missing, reordered, non-finite, or label-contaminated feature.

## 6. V2.3 family models

Train a stacked eligibility classifier for every targeted family using the
original measured balanced-profile eligibility target. Do not change the target
to measured-best: the diagnostic showed that the existing ranking already selects
the best candidate whenever the eligibility gate admits a safe opportunity.

Use one preregistered model configuration:

- Random forest with 750 trees.
- Maximum depth 12.
- Minimum leaf size 3.
- Square-root feature sampling.
- Bootstrap sampling and balanced-subsample class weights.
- Deterministic seed `20260717`.

A targeted family is supported only if the combined calibration data contains at
least ten opportunity cases and at least ten non-opportunity cases. Otherwise it
uses constant abstention. This support check is applied after the frozen 384-case
suite completes and cannot be lowered.

Popcount loads its hash-verified V2.2 classifier unchanged. Variable shift remains
constant abstention. The final deployable V2.3 classifiers are fit on all valid V2,
V2.1, and V2.3 calibration rows only after policy selection.

## 7. Comparable grouped calibration

Judge V2.3 on the same 936-case V2/V2.1 calibration reference distribution used
by the V2.2 diagnostic. The targeted 384-case expansion supplies training support
but does not change the weight of families or cases in the calibration gate.

For each targeted family, create five deterministic outer folds grouped by the
936 reference topology signatures. For each fold:

1. Train on four reference folds plus every disjoint V2.3 expansion row.
2. Predict the held-out reference fold.
3. Never score an expansion row as part of the gate.

This produces exactly 2,808 grouped out-of-fold reference predictions comparable
case-for-case with V2.2. Reproduce the V2.2 diagnostic from those predictions and
persist both the old and new per-case classifications.

Report ROC AUC and average precision for candidate eligibility and case-level
opportunity in every family, but do not substitute those diagnostics for the
selective-risk gate.

## 8. Selective-risk and calibration gates

Reuse the V2.2 exact joint threshold optimizer and deterministic tie-break order.
Do not change its objective or loosen its constraints:

- Global harmful recommendation rate at most 5%.
- Global abstention specificity at least 90%.
- Global balanced actionable accuracy at least 70%.
- Every supported family with at least ten recommendations has harmful rate at
  most 10%.
- Every supported family has abstention specificity at least 80%.
- Unsupported families always abstain.

V2.3 must also satisfy these recovery checks on the same reference cases:

- At least 94 covered opportunities, eight more than V2.2.
- No fewer than 83 recommendations survive the frozen leave-one-topology-out OOD
  overlay, matching the V2.2 safe-coverage count.
- All selected reference candidates pass registered rule detection and preserve
  100% lint/formal acceptance.
- The V2.2 popcount decisions remain identical.

Fit the existing family median/IQR nearest-neighbor OOD algorithm on the combined
calibration features and version its bundle as V2.3. Do not tune its percentile
or distance function. Calibration reports must include both the frozen V2.1 OOD
overlay for comparison and the deployable V2.3 OOD result.

If no policy satisfies every gate, persist the complete diagnostic frontier and
stop. Do not create a blind suite, benchmark lock, or unseal record.

## 9. Targeted physical cross-check

If the calibration gate passes, run a generated V2.3 OpenROAD delta audit before
blind generation:

- Select two calibration cases from each of the seven targeted families using a
  frozen stable hash: one recommended opportunity and one correct abstention when
  both exist.
- Run `v0`-`v3` for 14 cases and 56 maximum OpenROAD runs under the pinned image,
  ORFS commit, library, clocks, utilization, and retry policy from the V2 physical
  lock.
- Require at least 12 complete cases, at least one complete case per targeted
  family, candidate-action agreement of at least 80%, and delay, area, and
  cell-count direction agreement of at least 75% each.
- Require routing completion, finite timing, and zero DRC violations for every
  usable run.

An unavailable recommended/abstention stratum is recorded and filled by the next
stable-hash case in that family; it is not a reason to inspect blind data. The
existing 108-run V2 report remains an immutable dependency and the delta report
is additive.

## 10. Advisor contract

Add separately versioned `analyze-v23` behavior that:

- Hash-verifies the V2.3 stacked schema, family bundle, OOD bundle, policy,
  calibration report, and physical reports.
- Computes base predictions and stacked features from live pre-synthesis RTL.
- Applies only the detected family's classifier and threshold.
- Preserves point, risk, and safe modes and existing fail-closed behavior.
- Preserves V2.1 candidate ranking after eligibility qualification.
- Preserves deterministic findings, source mapping, patch identity, lint, and
  equivalence checks.
- Rejects diagnostic-only or failed-gate artifacts.

Codex may explain the deterministic result, tradeoff, and supporting RTL facts.
It may not alter eligibility, ranking, abstention, or candidate content.

## 11. New blind suite and promotion

Only after both calibration and physical gates pass, generate
`corpus/heldout-v23` with deterministic seed `20260717`: eight cases per family,
72 cases total. Every topology signature must be disjoint from V2, V2.1,
calibration-v23, and every prior held-out suite. Freeze RTL and descriptor hashes
before prevalidation.

The held-out suite must pass lint, positive formal equivalence, and negative
control rejection with synthesis disabled. Blind synthesis is allowed only after
an immutable V2.3 benchmark lock and explicit unseal record exist.

Use the V2.2 benchmark and promotion contract unchanged: 480 records, 264 GPT-5.6
Sol xhigh calls, six conceptual arms, exact call accounting, no cached
substitutions, deterministic retries, and all micro, macro, opportunity,
specificity, harmful-rate, direction, exact-best, regret, formal-yield,
reliability, stability, and physical-evidence gates. A blind failure ends V2.3;
any post-blind correction requires V2.4 and a newly generated blind suite.

## 13. Implementation sequence

### Phase 1 — Freeze the plan and generator

- Persist this plan and its SHA-256.
- Implement expanded domains, fixed allocations, propensity selection, blind
  reserve checks, and topology-disjointness tests.
- Freeze `calibration-v23` descriptors before any RTL or labels are generated.

### Phase 2 — Generate trustworthy evidence

- Generate all 384 cases.
- Run lint, formal equivalence, negative controls, and synthesis.
- Freeze the validated suite, training rows, tool versions, and input lock.

### Phase 3 — Implement the stacked model

- Build and provenance-check the stacked feature table.
- Produce grouped reference out-of-fold predictions.
- Fit family classifiers, refit the versioned OOD bundle, and run the unchanged
  joint threshold optimizer.
- Persist per-case failure deltas and all score-separation diagnostics.

### Phase 4 — Enforce pre-blind gates

- Stop if calibration fails.
- If calibration passes, run and freeze the 56-run maximum OpenROAD delta audit.
- Stop if physical evidence fails.

### Phase 5 — Integrate and evaluate

- Implement and test V2.3 live analysis and lock verification.
- Only then generate and formally prevalidate `heldout-v23`.
- Lock, unseal once, execute the fixed blind benchmark, and promote only if every
  gate passes.

## 14. Operating boundaries

- Use only generated or explicitly approved open RTL; never use company or
  proprietary RTL.
- Never modify source RTL in place.
- Never use synthesis, timing, physical, case-ID, or split labels at live
  inference time.
- Never use any held-out label for training, feature selection, model selection,
  threshold selection, or implementation repair.
- Never relax a gate after observing its result.
- Never let Codex override the deterministic advisor.
- Never bypass failed lint, formal, calibration, OOD, physical, lock, or blind
  checks.
