# RTL Advisor V2.2 Implementation Plan

## 1. Objective

V2.2 is a policy-only recovery iteration for the failed V2.1 eligibility gate. It
keeps the V2.1 feature schema, registered rules, regression models, direction
classifiers, OOD model, balanced PPA profile, candidate ranking, formal
verification contract, and passing OpenROAD evidence unchanged. It replaces only
the monolithic eligibility classifier and global risk threshold with a
family-aware selective-risk policy.

V2.1 remains immutable and unpromoted. V2.2 uses separately versioned code and
artifacts and does not become the live default unless every calibration, physical,
and blind gate in this document passes.

## 2. Frozen evidence and allowed inputs

V2.2 may use only calibration evidence that was already available before any
blind unseal:

- The 2,808 V2.1 calibration candidate rows across 936 topology groups.
- The V2.1 grouped out-of-fold regression and direction predictions.
- V2 and V2.1 calibration synthesis labels.
- The V2.1 kernel-only feature schema and family OOD model.
- The passing V2 OpenROAD physical report.

V2.2 must verify and record the hashes of all reused inputs. It may not read or
generate synthesis labels for `heldout-v21` or the future `heldout-v22` suite
during policy development. The frozen V2 and V2.1 artifacts may not be
overwritten.

## 3. Family-aware eligibility models

Train one eligibility classifier per optimization family using the frozen V2.1
candidate features and measured balanced-profile eligibility labels.

- Random forest with 500 trees.
- Maximum depth 12.
- Minimum leaf size 3.
- Square-root feature sampling.
- Bootstrap sampling and balanced-subsample class weights.
- Deterministic seed `20260716`.
- Five-fold grouped out-of-fold predictions by topology signature within each
  family.

A family with fewer than ten calibration opportunity cases is unsupported and
must use a constant-abstain policy. This support floor is frozen before model
evaluation and prevents a small number of positive cases from creating an
unstable deployable policy.

The final deployable family classifiers are fit on all calibration rows only
after the grouped out-of-fold policy is selected.

## 4. Joint selective-risk calibration

For each supported family, enumerate candidate thresholds from that family's
grouped out-of-fold eligibility probabilities plus `0.0` and `1.0`. Evaluate
every threshold at case level:

1. Qualify candidates whose family probability clears the threshold.
2. Rank qualified candidates with the unchanged V2.1 balanced-utility
   regressors.
3. Record opportunity coverage, abstention specificity, recommendations, and
   harmful recommendations.

Choose exactly one threshold per family with a deterministic joint optimizer.
The objective is maximum global opportunity recall, followed in order by higher
balanced actionable accuracy, higher abstention specificity, lower harmful rate,
and lexicographically higher family thresholds.

The selected policy is feasible only when all of these calibration constraints
hold:

- Global harmful recommendation rate at most 5%.
- Global abstention specificity at least 90%.
- Global balanced actionable accuracy at least 70%.
- Every supported family with at least ten recommendations has harmful rate at
  most 10%.
- Every supported family has abstention specificity at least 80%.
- Unsupported families always abstain.

Persist the complete threshold frontier, selected family thresholds, aggregate
metrics, per-family metrics, optimizer inputs, deterministic tie-break evidence,
and a policy hash. If no joint policy is feasible, retain the safest diagnostic
frontier and stop before blind-suite generation.

## 5. Reused direction, ranking, OOD, and physical contracts

V2.2 does not retrain or retune the V2.1 regression, direction, or OOD stack.
Their bundle, feature, policy, and metadata hashes are dependencies of the V2.2
model lock.

The V2.1 grouped-OOF direction gates remain required:

- At least 70% accuracy at at least 90% conditional coverage overall.
- At least 60% accuracy separately for delay, area, and cell count.

The physical dependency is the frozen passing OpenROAD report with semantic hash:

```text
6dcb060959df8e41f1c919fe794b5631f6d3b055759febab88e1fe16692f0643
```

No OpenROAD rerun is required because V2.2 changes only the pre-synthesis
eligibility policy, not RTL generation, PPA labels, ranking utility, or physical
interpretation.

## 6. V2.2 advisor contract

Add separately versioned V2.2 analysis that:

- Loads and hash-verifies the V2.2 family eligibility bundle and policy.
- Loads and hash-verifies the reused V2.1 regressors, direction classifiers, and
  OOD model.
- Selects the eligibility classifier and threshold only from the detected
  transformation family.
- Uses constant abstention for unsupported families.
- Preserves the V2.1 `point`, `risk`, and `safe` modes.
- Preserves deterministic candidate identity, ranking, source mapping, candidate
  emission, lint, and formal verification behavior.
- Keeps Codex explanation-only and unable to change the deterministic decision.

V2.2 analysis must reject diagnostic-only models whose calibration policy is not
feasible.

## 7. New blind suite

Only after the V2.2 calibration policy passes, generate:

```text
corpus/heldout-v22
```

Use deterministic seed `20260716` and create eight cases per family, 72 cases
total. Every topology signature must be disjoint from V2, V2.1 calibration,
V2.1 held-out, and any V2.2 calibration input.

Freeze descriptors and RTL hashes before validation. Require Verilator lint,
formal equivalence for `v1`–`v3`, and formal rejection of `n0`. Do not synthesize
the suite until a V2.2 benchmark lock exists and an explicit unseal record is
written.

## 8. Benchmark and promotion contract

The V2.2 blind benchmark retains the corrected V2.1 scoring definitions and six
conceptual arms, replacing the V2.1 model arms with V2.2:

1. V1 registered rules.
2. V1 Codex advisor.
3. V1 hybrid.
4. V2.2 point predictor.
5. V2.2 family-risk gate.
6. V2.2 safe advisor.

Retain 480 benchmark records, 264 GPT-5.6 Sol xhigh calls, exact run/call plans,
no cached substitutions, deterministic retries, and immutable lock verification.

Promotion requires every V2.1 blind target unchanged:

- Micro balanced actionable accuracy at least 70%.
- Macro balanced actionable accuracy at least 65%.
- Opportunity coverage at least 50%.
- Abstention specificity at least 90%.
- Harmful recommendation rate at most 10%.
- At least a 10 percentage-point improvement over V1 hybrid, with the paired 95%
  bootstrap lower bound above zero.
- Direction accuracy at least 70% at conditional coverage of at least 90%.
- Direction accuracy at least 60% separately for delay, area, and cell count.
- Tie-aware exact-best accuracy at least 60%.
- Conditional normalized ranking regret at most 0.10.
- Accepted-candidate lint and formal pass rate of 100%.
- Candidate emission yield at least 80%.
- Deterministic decision and candidate stability of 100%.
- Advisor schema and reliability success at least 98%.
- Advisor semantic stability at least 85%.
- The frozen OpenROAD physical-evidence gate remains hash-valid and passing.

Failure of any blind gate is reported without post-blind retuning. A correction
after blind evaluation requires a new version and a newly generated blind suite.

## 9. Versioned artifacts and commands

Write new model artifacts under:

```text
artifacts/models/v22
```

Add versioned commands:

```text

rtl-advisor analyze-v22
rtl-advisor corpus generate-suite-v22 --split heldout-v22
rtl-advisor corpus validate-suite-v22 --split heldout-v22
rtl-advisor benchmark lock-v22
rtl-advisor benchmark run-v22
rtl-advisor benchmark report-v22
```

Benchmark artifacts are written under `artifacts/benchmarks/v22`. No V2.2 blind
lock or unseal artifact may be created while calibration is infeasible.

## 10. Implementation sequence

### Phase 1 — Freeze and verify

- Persist this plan and its hash.
- Verify reused V2.1 model, calibration, feature, and physical-report hashes.
- Add tests proving V2 and V2.1 artifacts remain unchanged.

### Phase 2 — Implement family risk

- Implement family grouped-OOF classifiers.
- Implement deterministic joint threshold optimization and diagnostic fallback.
- Persist family/aggregate calibration evidence and immutable model metadata.

### Phase 3 — Integrate the advisor and lock gates

- Implement V2.2 bundle loading and family policy selection.
- Preserve V2.1 prediction, direction, OOD, ranking, emission, and verification
  semantics.
- Require passing calibration and physical dependencies before blind locking.

### Phase 4 — Calibrate

- Train V2.2 only from frozen calibration inputs.
- Produce the calibration and family-risk reports.
- Stop if any pre-blind gate fails.

### Phase 5 — Blind evaluation, only if eligible

- Generate and formally prevalidate the disjoint V2.2 blind suite.
- Freeze the V2.2 benchmark lock before synthesis.
- Unseal and run the fixed benchmark once.
- Promote only if every target passes; otherwise retain the current default.

## 11. Operating boundaries

- Use only generated or explicitly approved open RTL.
- Never use company or proprietary RTL.
- Never modify source RTL in place.
- Never use post-synthesis or physical labels as live inference features.
- Never use V2, V2.1, or V2.2 blind labels for training or threshold selection.
- Never allow Codex to override a deterministic decision.
- Never bypass a failed calibration, physical, formal, or blind gate.
